The Philippine peso is on the brink of reaching a new record-low against the U.S. dollar.

On May 23, the Philippine currency pierced through the 58 to a dollar level for the first time in two years. The following day, it hit 58.13 against the greenback.

While the dollar has lost some of its purchasing power domestically, it has increased in value against major international currencies. For instance, for the first time in 20 years, the dollar achieved rate parity against the Euro.
